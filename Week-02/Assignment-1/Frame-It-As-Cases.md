# Case Study 1 — AI Fluency Internship

## The Problem

When I started the AI Fluency internship, I mostly used AI like a search engine—I asked questions and accepted the answers. I didn't know how to collaborate with AI to solve problems, improve workflows, or document my work effectively.

I wanted to move beyond simple question-and-answer interactions and learn how AI is actually used in real engineering work.

---

## What I Did

I completed the Week 1 AI Fluency assignments, including:

- AI Workflow Audit
- Portfolio Sitemap
- Proof Statement
- AI Workspace setup

While working through these assignments, I changed how I interacted with AI.

Instead of asking one-off questions, I started giving AI context, defining goals, breaking work into smaller tasks, and iterating until the output matched what I actually wanted.

The biggest mindset shift came when I saw AI automate complete workflows with very little human intervention once the rules and instructions were clearly defined. That was the moment I realized AI is much more than a chatbot—it can become a reliable collaborator.

I also learned an equally important lesson: AI is not always right. Sometimes it confidently provides incorrect information or makes assumptions. Instead of accepting the first answer, I now verify important information, refine my prompts, and iterate until I'm confident in the final result.

---

## Outcome

By the end of Week 1, my workflow had completely changed.

Instead of only asking questions, I now use AI to:

- Plan projects
- Improve documentation
- Structure ideas
- Automate repetitive work
- Learn faster while keeping human judgment in the loop

The biggest lesson I learned is that great AI results come from clear instructions, thoughtful iteration, and careful verification.

# Case Study 2 — Machine Learning Internship

## The Problem

I wanted to understand how machine learning is applied to real business problems instead of only learning algorithms from tutorials.

I also wanted experience working with real datasets and understanding how decisions are made during an ML project.

---

## What I Did

I completed the internship starter notebooks using anonymized SEO datasets.

While exploring the data, I investigated relationships between:

- Search Volume
- Impressions
- Click Through Rate (CTR)
- Ranking Position
- Word Count

I also selected my research direction around **Content Refresh and SEO Optimization**, where I explored how content characteristics relate to declining page performance.

Some important findings included:

- Search volume alone has almost no correlation with impressions.
- CTR decreases significantly as ranking positions drop.
- Longer articles do not automatically perform better.
- Simple Decision Trees can create understandable rules for business decisions.

Beyond the notebooks, I learned how to evaluate machine learning models using metrics like Precision@K and how to interpret models instead of treating them as black boxes.

---

## Outcome

The internship helped me understand that machine learning is much more than training models.

It starts with asking the right business question, understanding the data, evaluating the results, and deciding whether the model actually solves a useful problem.

I now have a much stronger understanding of practical machine learning workflows.
